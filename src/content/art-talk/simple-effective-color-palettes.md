When confronted with all the beautiful PanPastel colors, it can be hard to decide what to buy. Here is the good news: you can create gorgeous palettes with just three colors — a yellow, a red, and a blue.

## The Three-Color Foundation

To be sure you paint correct values, it's a good idea in the long run to get all four values of each color (the standard, plus tints and shades). But to start, three well-chosen hues will take you very far.

**Color temperature matters.** A warm yellow (like Yellow Ochre) will mix very differently than a cool yellow (like Hansa Yellow). A warm red (Pyrrole Red) gives different results than a cool red (Quinacridone Magenta). Choosing your temperature intentionally is the first step to a coherent palette.

## Example Palettes

- **Warm Palette:** Pyrrole Red, Cadmium Yellow, Ultramarine Blue
- **Cool Palette:** Quinacridone Magenta, Lemon Yellow, Phthalo Blue
- **Earthy Palette:** Burnt Sienna, Yellow Ochre, Prussian Blue
- **Neutral Palette:** Raw Umber, Yellow Ochre, Payne's Grey
- **Zorn Palette:** Yellow Ochre, Pyrrole Red, Ivory Black (and white) — named after the Swedish painter Anders Zorn

## Why Color Charts Are Essential

Before you paint with any color, make a chart. Put down each color at full strength, then mix it with its neighbors, then add white. You will see immediately how the colors behave together — whether they make beautiful grays and neutrals, or whether they fight each other.

This ten-minute investment saves hours of frustration in actual paintings.
