Since writing my earlier piece on pastel pencils, I have had the pleasure of trying a new brand — and it has moved immediately to the top of my list.

## Koh-I-Noor Gioconda Pastel Pencils

I ordered these through Dakota Art Pastels online, and I am very glad I did. The Gioconda pencils are a revelation.

**What makes them exceptional:**

- **Rich, saturated pigment.** The colors are deep and vibrant — considerably more so than many competitors at this price point.
- **Smooth, even application.** The core goes down without skipping, dragging, or depositing crumbs.
- **Excellent sharpenability.** Unlike the Caran d'Ache (which I love but find treacherous to sharpen), the Gioconda pencils sharpen cleanly and hold a good point.

**Color notes:**

- *Steel Grey* — a beautiful, cool neutral that mixes well with everything
- *Mouse Grey* — warmer, more useful than you might expect
- *Light Grey* — good for highlights and atmosphere
- *Light Orange* — surprisingly versatile, warm and lively
- *Sienna* — a rich, deep earth tone
- *Indian Red* — a classic warm red with good transparency
- *Paris Blue* — a muted, slightly grayed blue, wonderful for shadows
- *Ultramarine Medium* — the go-to blue for most work

**Where to buy:**

Dakota Art Pastels carries a full range and ships reliably. Well worth the order.

**Bottom line:** If you have been looking for a pastel pencil that performs as beautifully as it looks in the tin, try the Koh-I-Noor Gioconda. It is now my first recommendation to students.
